I am a California native, born and raised in San Diego. I am currently a PhD student in computational neuroscience working in the department of psychosomatics, psychotherapy and psychiatry at the university hospital at RWTH Aachen University in Aachen, Germany. 
<br>
I am super interested in anything brain-related and learning more about data-driven methods. I currently focus on Bayesian probabilistic modelling and machine learning. I first dabbled in programming at ten years old when left alone to my own advances, created a rudimentary pokemon website with my sister to give tips to other ten year olds. That was where I learned about HTML and CSS and online bulletin board systems (BBS). Fast forward to moving to Germany for my master's (hello free tuition!), I learned R and was hooked. Since starting my PhD, I predominantly use Python. Needless to say, I am super happy with all the cool things it can do and try to convince my friends that Python is amazing. 
<br>
Besides that, most of my life consists of brewing the right cup of coffee, travel, browsing interior design blogs and anything DIY. 
<br>
If you happen to live in Nordrhein Westfalia, check out NEURD, an organization for undergrad and graduate students in the fields of computational neuroscience, cognitive psychology, and related fields. NEURD is a platform where we can exchange and discuss ideas and projects, provide support for each other and network with other early career scientists.
